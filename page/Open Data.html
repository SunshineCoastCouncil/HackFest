Open Data - Dataset Listing

Data sets/APIs:

Below is a list of data sets which may be used in the Hackfest competition.

Open data from other sources can be used, providing it is data that is publicly available and not subject to privacy or copyright legislation.

The competition details such as themes or solutions to create won’t be launched until just prior to Hackfest 2016, but you are welcome to view and play with the data early so you can make the most of the Hackfest weekend.

Additional Sunshine Coast Council publicly available open data sets may be requested and if available, may be able to be provided subject to data owners approval.

Other data:

Australian

  • The data.gov.au portal for data from all Australian governments.
  • National Map provides a mapping service auto-generated from data.gov.au,
  • FIND is a catalogue of Australian spatial data sets which queries data from Australian federal, state and territory governments as well as research organisations.
  • ASGS (ABS) – Statistical administrative boundaries – mesh blocks, etc.
  • Bureau of Meterology - Stream level and flow web services (BoM), Geofabric web services (BoM)
  • The data.act.gov.au portal for ACT government data.
  • The data.nsw.gov.au portal for NSW government data.
  • NSW Globe, a mapping and data application for exploring spatial data and spatially referenced NSW open data.
  • The data.sa.gov.au South Australian government open data
  • The data.vic.gov.au portal for Victorian government open data.
  • The City of Melbourne open data portal
  • The Shared Land Information Platform (SLIP), which gives users access to Western Australia’s significant land and geographic information resources over the web.

Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW)

AIHW has a number of data sets related to Australia’s health and welfare available on the data.gov.au site

  • General Record of Incidence of Mortality (GRIM) books – Extracted in machine readable form from the AIHW
  • General Record of Incidence of Mortality (GRIM) books
  • Australian Cancer Incidence and Mortality – Extracted in machine readable form from the AIHW
  • Australian Cancer Incidence and Mortality books. These files contain summary statistics by age, year and sex for major cancers.
  • Youth detention population in Australia 2013
  • National Drugs Strategy Household Survey
  • Intercountry Adoptions in Australia
  • Health expenditure in Australia
  • Australian hospital statistics 2012-13
