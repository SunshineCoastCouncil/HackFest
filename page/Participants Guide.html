To be eligible for the Hackfest 2016 competition, your entry must only contain code and APIs that are available under an open license that allows re-sue, commercial use, remixing and redistribution. What can I submit?

HackFest is all about taking something and making it better. Using “open data”, data that is officially and freely available for use and reuse, you have to create something clever for one or more of the HackFest categories.

Entries could be one of the following or could be an idea that is not listed below…Please check with HackFest organisers if you would like confirmation of what is eligible.
- an app, a website, a digital sign, an online game, internet of things (IOT), a digital sensor display, a 3D model, a visualisation of data. If you are unsure whether your entry is suitable, please check this with the HackFest team .

Categories and Themes will be nominated from the many ideas that have been submitted as part of the HackFest IdeasFest.

Your entries can be concepts, prototypes, mockups, models or design concepts. Due to the timeframes you may not be able to complete a complex app. However your proof of concept should be the best effort that you can achieve to showcase a workable solution that you can demonstrate. Your proof of concept should be showcased in some way in your video submission.

Eligibility for Prizes

To be eligible for prizes, individual entrants must be either an Australian or a current Australian resident (this includes temporary student residents). For team entrants, at least one member of the team must be an Australian citizen or a current Australian resident (this includes temporary student residents). At least one team member must be over 18 or a guardian must be registered as the representative to facilitate prize collection .

Entries submitted should be primarily developed over the weekend of the HackFest event.

If an entry is found to be created prior to the event, the Sunshine Coast Council HackFest organisers may choose to not include this entry in the prize category.. An existing solution may be enhanced and entered in a prize category however the details of this entry must be clearly specified in the entry details.

Team Criteria. Entrants may be members of multiple teams but each team must be registered separately

Sunshine Coast Council employees – Sunshine Coast Council employees including contractors & casual employees, are only eligible to enter the category(s) that have been specifically nominated for these participants. Prizes or recognition awards will be provided.

Sunshine Coast Council employees who attend and participate in the HackFest 2016 event will also be required to assist any participant or volunteer with questions about data or other information, keeping with the collaborative, open and sharing culture of the HackFest 2016 event.

Nominated judges of HackFest 2016 will not be eligible to compete for prizes, and individuals from organisations or companies are also not eligible for prizes sponsored by their organisation. Mentors/volunteers or any other participants are eligible to compete for prizes, but Sunshine Coast Council HackFest organisers reserve the right to disqualify a mentor/speaker if they perceive unfairness.

Submitting your entry

An online repository will be available for you to upload your entry for Hackfest 2016. In order to compete for the Hackfest prizes, you will have to follow these instructions.

  • Make sure all of your team members are registered.
  • Upload a project page for each prize that you compete for. This must include
    • Your team name and your team members,
    • The name of your entry,
    • A description of your entry,
    • What data has been used to create your entry, providing a link to the dataset locations
    • How you have used the data
    • An image or logo that relates to your entry.
    • What competition category you are entering

Include your entry content. This must include all

  • Code , graphics, applications, website URLs, and any other components relevant to your entry
  • All components of the entry must be all made available under an open source/content license to be eligible for prizes. Teams can put the code/source in an external repository system and make the URL available on their team page for verification
  • A video that show your entry in action for the judging.
    • Please demonstrate how your solution works, how it meets the criteria of the category and how it can provide benefits or positive outcomes for the community or intended audience. Please ensure that you explain your solution in a way that non-technical judges will be able to understand. You can be creative with your submission including interviews of community members explaining what the issue is, outdoor footage showing your solution in action etc
    • You are required to showcase your entry in the video and demonstrate how it relates to the category and the solution required.

Code of Conduct

By participating in HackFest 2016, as an observer or a competitor, you agree to the following:

I will respect and participate in the spirit of the Hackfest event
I will endeavor to make the event as enjoyable for myself and all other participants and organisers. I will treat all participants, organisers and visitors to the event with courtesy. I will not swear or harass other participants or make offensive comments or exclusionary jokes related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion. I will behave in a way that is suitable for anyone to view, including young children.

I will respect the venue and equipment
I will keep the venue clean and tidy and use the rubbish and recycling bins as appropriate. I will cooperate with the event organizers as required and report any issues. Smoking is strictly prohibited in the event venue and adjacent facilities.

I shall follow the competition rules
I will only use authorised materials that I have the right to use and release and will not submit any projects which are potentially libelous, false, defamatory or overtly political or contains material which is potentially confidential, commercially sensitive, or which would cause personal distress or loss. I will check the website and/or have a chat with one of the organisers if at any stage I am unclear on the competition rules.

I shall look after others and myself
I will remember that we are all here to create, not party. I will not attend the event intoxicated or drink excessively at the event. If I am under 18 years of age, I will follow the law in regard to no consumption of alcohol.

And remember that the HackFest organisers & volunteers are in charge and have put a lot of effort in to creating a great event
I will not do anything to ruin it or engage in any behavior that violates this code of conduct. I understand that the HackFest organizers can take any action they deem appropriate, including warnings or expulsion from the HackFest event. I understand that if I am removed from the event due to inappropriate behavior, I will no longer be eligible to compete for HackFest prizes.

Competition Judging Criteria

Below is a listing of what will be the judging criteria for HackFest 2016.

Please consider this when submitting your entry to ensure you have the best possible chance of being rewarded for your efforts. This is an outline only and may be subject to change at the Official HackFest 2016 discretion.

Judging Criteria - Prizes 1st, 2nd or 3rd

  • Quality and design
  • The value this solution would be to the community or business users
  • Consistency with contest purposes including social value, community or business value
  • The creation and formation of the entry, including the complexity, code, and other effort that has been undertaken to complete this submission
  • Usability (including documentation and ease of use)
  • The relevance of the solution to the project definition as described in the project overview document submitted by the team – eg does the solution solve an issue, does the solution work…
  • The identified use of open data, sources (or possible sources) and how that relates to needs or opportunities specific to the Sunshine Coast.
  • Would the solution be useful to its intended audience,
  • The ability to use this solution or take this solution to a commercial outcome
  • The ability to expand this solution to add more features at a later time.

Category Prizes

  • Is the solution relevant to the category eg community , business or creativity using open data category definition
  • Useability
  • Relevance and value as a solution
  • Value to the community, business or as an Open Data initiative.
